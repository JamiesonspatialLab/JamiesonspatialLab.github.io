About the lab

Who we are

The Jamieson Spatial Lab is part of the School of Cancer Sciences within the College of Medical, Veterinary and Life Sciences at the University of Glasgow. Led by Professor Nigel Jamieson, we are a multidisciplinary team applying spatial biology to understand the role of tissue architecture in health and disease.

We combine spatial transcriptomics, spatial proteomics, and multiplexed tissue imaging to study how different cell types are organised within tissues — and how disruption of that organisation underpins a wide range of diseases, from cancer to chronic inflammation. By mapping cells in their native spatial context, we uncover biological insights that are simply invisible to conventional approaches.

We are committed to open, reproducible science and work in close partnership with clinical colleagues across NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde. Our access to world-class spatial platforms and linked biorepositories makes us uniquely placed to translate spatial biology into clinically meaningful discovery.
